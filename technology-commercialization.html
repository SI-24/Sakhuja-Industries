TECHNOLOGY COMMERCIALIZATION

The Bridge Between Research, Industry, and Deployment.

SIPL connects the innovation originating in India's research institutions to the manufacturing capacity of industry, and carries it through to end users who need it deployed — converting scientific promise into operational capability.

THE BRIDGE MODEL

One Connective Layer, Four Stakeholders

Research and industry rarely speak the same operational language. SIPL sits across that divide — translating lab-stage innovation into manufacturable, deployable, and adopted capability.

NODE 01

Research Institutions

Originators of indigenous IP, prototypes, and deep-tech breakthroughs.

NODE 02

Industry

Strategic and commercial players seeking validated, deployable technology.

NODE 03

Manufacturing Partners

Production-capable partners who scale technology into supply.

NODE 04

End Users

Institutional and strategic adopters who deploy the finished capability.

SIPL — operating across all four nodes as the evaluation, integration, and commercialization layer that keeps technology moving forward.
THE COMMERCIALIZATION JOURNEY

From Laboratory Result to Fielded Capability

Every technology SIPL takes on moves through a disciplined journey — each stage designed to reduce risk and build confidence for the stakeholder waiting at the next node.

01

Discovery & Scouting

SIPL identifies promising technologies within research institutions, incubators, and innovator networks — screened against strategic and industrial relevance.

02

Technical & Commercial Validation

The technology is assessed for scientific soundness, IP standing, manufacturability, and market or strategic demand before any commercialization commitment is made.

03

Structuring the Transfer

SIPL structures the technology transfer or licensing pathway between the research institution and the industry or manufacturing partner, protecting IP and aligning incentives.

04

Manufacturing Readiness

The technology is engineered for production — tooling, processes, and supply chains are built or aligned to take it from prototype to repeatable manufacture.

05

Industrial Scale-Up

Manufacturing partners scale production, with SIPL coordinating quality, capacity, and delivery commitments across the supply chain.

06

Deployment to End Users

The finished capability reaches institutional, strategic, or commercial end users — closing the loop from research to real-world impact.

TECHNOLOGY TRANSFER OPPORTUNITIES

Pathways for Research Institutions to Commercialize IP

SIPL works with research institutions and innovators to structure the right transfer pathway for the technology, its IP position, and its readiness stage.

01

IP Licensing

Structured licensing of patented or patent-pending technology to industry and manufacturing partners for commercial deployment.

02

Co-Development Agreements

Joint development arrangements where SIPL and the research institution refine a technology toward manufacturing readiness together.

03

Prototype-to-Product Transition

Converting lab-validated prototypes into engineered, production-ready designs suited for industrial manufacture.

04

Sponsored Technology Evaluation

Assessment engagements where SIPL evaluates a technology's commercial and strategic viability on behalf of an industry partner.

05

Spin-Out Enablement

Supporting researchers and innovators in structuring a commercialization vehicle around their technology.

06

Strategic Technology Adoption

Fast-tracking technologies with strategic or national relevance toward institutional adoption.
